FPR-researcher of the week

FRANCESCO CONTI

Who is Francesco Conti?

  • Francesco Conti is currently a Junior Assistant Professor (RTD-A) in the DEI department of the University of Bologna.
  • Previously, has been a postdoctoral researcher at the Integrated Systems Laboratory
  • His main works were into heterogeneous multicore architectures, in particular for energy-efficient and low-power embedded systems.
  • Educational Qualification:
  • He completed his Bachelor’s and Master’s in Electronics Engineering from University of Bologna at Italy
  • ⁍ Also as a part of his Master’s he had been to Delft University of Technology - Netherlands on an Erasmus exchange program

Projects:

  • ⁍ He is one of the main contributors of the open-source PULP platform project [http://pulp-platform.org] in particular for what concerns the integration of specialized hardware accelerators into the platform
  • ⁍ Also he developed pytorch scripts to train mixed-precision networks for microcontroller deployment, based on the memory contraints of the target device.

Publications:

  • XNOR Neural Engine: a Hardware Accelerator IP for 21.6 fJ/op Binary Neural Network Inference - Oct 2018
  • Accelerated Visual Context Classification on a Low-Power Smartwatch - 2017
  • A Ultra-Low-Energy Convolution Engine for Fast Brain-Inspired Vision in Multicore Clusters - Mar 11, 2015.
  • An IoT Endpoint System-on-Chip for Secure and Energy-Efficient Near-Sensor Analytics - 2017

Research Interests:

  • ⁍ Architectures for Parallel Acceleration
  • ⁍ Parallel ultra-low power platforms and development of HW accelerators for enhanced energy efficiency.
  • ⁍ Heterogeneous Multicore Shared Memory Cluster for Embedded Computer Vision Applications

Contributions:

  • ⁍ Research Paper on “XNOR Neural Engine: a Hardware Accelerator IP for 21.6 fJ/op Binary Neural Network Inference”
  • ⁍ Successful technology transfer (via licensing) of a Ultra-Low Energy Hardware Convolution Engine (HWCE)
  • ⁍ Development of a fault-injection methodology to study cross-layer resilience techniques on a parallel ultra-low power platform.

Awards:

  • 25th IEEE International Conference Best Paper Award at ASAP - 2014
